Popular Nigerian actor Jerry Amilo has addressed his reasons for uploading the remains of his late colleague Junior Pope.

Legit.ng reported that the young, vibrant actor was involved in a tragic marine accident that took his life and that of other crew members on board.

Following the sad incident, the veteran who was spotted in the last hospital that declared Pope dead, alongside AGN president Emeka Rollas, shared a clip of the deceased, lifeless body on the hospital bed.

Some celebrities and fans berated Amilo for revealing such to the public.

In response to the agitation, Jerry shared his reasons for uploading the Junior Pope's corpse.

According to him, he wanted people to see the truth for themselves, considering the back-and-forth reports surrounding the mishap.

Jerry noted that he didn't put up the video for clout and claimed that he has passed that stage where he would belittle himself to social media attention.

In a chat with Legit.ng, Sunny Okonkwo, an award-winning actor and movie producer, discussed the dynamics of social media in Nigeria, using Jerry Amilo's actions as an example to illustrate its harmful impact.

He also emphasised the need for genuine human connection and empathy in an increasingly digital world.

"With a good and bad intent of social media currently in Nigeria and how the perception has changed, Jerry Amilo's actions shed light on the detrimental impact of social media in Nigeria, where the pursuit of virality often overshadows empathy and compassion."The constant craving for attention has desensitized many to the suffering of others, leading them to prioritize going viral over showing genuine concern. This trend reflects a broader societal shift where genuine human connection is sacrificed for the sake of online validation and clout. "He may have actually just wanted to put the truth out since there were varied information flying online, but you know we already have cases where celebrities and other content creators do this to chase clout and gain following." If he did it with good intentions, he shouldn't bother explaining because he will explain tire no evidence; the evidence is his heart."
