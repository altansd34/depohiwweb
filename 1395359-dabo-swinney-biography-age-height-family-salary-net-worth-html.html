His career as a football coach

Upon completing his studies, William joined the Crimson Tide as one of their full-time coaches. His role was to guide the team's wide receivers and tight ends.

Unfortunately, in 2001, the team sacked the then-head coach Mike DuBose plus his backroom staff members. Throughout the 2001 season, he sat out without any coaching responsibilities.

He was offered a job in Birmingham-based AIG Baker Real Estate by his former coach Rich Wingo. He accepted the offer and worked at the company for two years.

Dabo Swinney contract

On August 20th, 2017, Dabo penned down what could be described as the largest college football contract ever. He signed an eight-year $54 million contract with Clemson.

According to the contract terms, Dabo Swinney salary was at least $8.25 million during that year's season. His salary would increase to $10 million in 2027.

Additionally, the contract includes other incentives like a $250,000 bonus for a national title, $200,000 bonus for an ACC championship and $50,000 if he wins a coach of the year award.

Awards and achievements

For the years he has been at the helm, Swinney has won numerous titles and awards for the Clemson.

  • In 2011, he won the Bobby Dodd Coach of the Year Award.
  • In 2015 and 2018, he won the ACC Coach of the Year Awards.
  • He has won eight ACC Atlantic Division in 2009, 2011, 2012, and 2015 to 2019.
  • He has won three Paul ‘Bear’ Bryant Award in 2015, 2016, and 2018.
  • In 2015, he won the AFCA Coach of the Year Award, the AP College Football Coach of the Year Award, and the Home Depot Coach of the Year Award.
  • He has won two National Championships in 2016 and 2018.
  • He has won five ACC Championships in 2011 and 2015 to 2018.
  • In 2015, he won the George Munger Award.
