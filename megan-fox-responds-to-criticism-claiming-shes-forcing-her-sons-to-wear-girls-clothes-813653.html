Megan Fox Responds to Criticism Claiming Shes Forcing Her Sons to Wear Girls Clothes

 Posted on August 28, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  422 words  |  Tandra Barner

Megan Fox just showed that no one should mess with her kids, unless they want to deal with her. The Transformers star didn’t hold back when she tore to shreds allegations that she has any influence at her three sons’ fashion choices. Let’s see what exactly happened and how Fox handled the upsetting situation.

Megan’s oldest son has always been into dresses.

PictureGroup/Sipa USA/East News

Fox once revealed that her oldest son, Noah, was really into fashion and, in particular, dresses: “Sometimes he’ll dress himself, and he likes to wear dresses sometimes.” For the record, Megan Fox shares three sons with her ex-husband Brian Austin Green: Noah, Bodhi, and Journey, born in 2012, 2014, and 2016, respectively.

The actress anticipated that since Noah is a boy, his fashion choices might be met with teasing, so she did everything in her power to protect him: “I sent him to a really liberal hippie school, but even there, here in California, he still has little boys going, ’Boys don’t wear dresses,’ or, ’Boys don’t wear pink.’ So we’re going through that now where I’m trying to teach him to be confident no matter what anyone else says.”

She was accused of making her sons wear girly things.

Megan and her ex-husband still continue to support Noah and their other sons, but it did find opposition from people online. A Twitter user Robby Starbuck shared that he personally knew the family and claimed that the boys’ appearance was controlled by Megan.

DIGITAL/EROTEME.CO.UK/East News

Starbuck attached a photo of Fox with her three boys, all of which can be seen having long hair, while Noah was also wearing a pink outfit with “strong girls” written on it. He also wrote: “These are Megan Fox’s sons. We used to live in the same gated community and our kids played at the park. I saw 2 of them have a full on breakdown, saying they were forced by their mom to wear girls’ clothes as their nanny tried to console them.”

Fox screenshotted Starbuck’s tweet and posted it on her Instagram, along with a response that would’ve made anyone shake in their boots. She accused Starbuck of trying to gain more popularity at her children’s expense and told him how low it was in her opinion: “Irregardless of how desperate you may become at any given time to acquire wealth, power, success, or fame — never use children as leverage or social currency.”

The boys’ father rushed to Megan’s defense.

But don’t just take Fox’s word for it, her ex-husband, Brian Austin Green, verified everything she said and supported her amid this situation. He said: “It’s a totally bogus story. There are only a few people in their world that can actually verify whether or not a story like this is true, and I can tell you with absolute certainty it is not.”

Then again, what difference does it make if a boy likes wearing girls’ clothing? There are many male celebrities, who do so, and look absolutely fabulous.
