Nigerian actress Uche Jombo's biography, career, and movies

 Posted on August 8, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1188 words  |  Patria Henriques

Uche Jombo Rodriguez, commonly known by her stage name Uche Jombo, is a Nigerian-born actress, screenwriter, film producer, and director. She is also the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Uche Jumbo Studios Production Company.

Uche's talent and skills have seen her reach the top in the much-acclaimed Nollywood entertainment industry. She is a highly sought after actresses, producer, director and screenwriter. Some of her most popular movies are Visa to Hell, Games Men Play and Kiss and Tell.

Uche Jombo biography

The actress was born on 28th December 1979 in Abiriba, Abia State, Nigeria. Uche was raised by her single mother, but she has not offered any information about her father and siblings.

Education

She attained her primary and secondary school education in Abiriba in Abia State. While in primary school, her mother enrolled her in the local church drama group.

In secondary school, Jombo always took home the best drama student award thanks to her exceptional talents. Her local church funded her entire secondary school education.

After completing her primary and secondary school studies, Uche enrolled at the University of Calabar for a degree in Mathematics and Statistics. She also has a second degree in Computer Programming from the Federal University of Technology Minna.

Career

Uche Jombo joined the film entertainment industry in 1999 with Visa to Hell as her debut film. As a screenwriter, Uche has written and co-written several Nollywood movies such as Girls in the Hood, Games Men Play and The Celebrity.

As a producer, the actress has produced the likes of Holding Hope, Nollywood Hustlers and Damage. She also launched her own film production company, Uche Jombo Studios Production Company, in 2008.

Later, Uche Jombo and husband welcomed their firstborn son named Mathew Chinedu Rodriguez. Uche Jombo baby was born on 27th May 2015. The family of three resides in the United States. However, the actress often travels back to Nigeria for work purposes.

After the birth of her baby, the actress added some weight and a few people took to social media to troll her. In response, she advised them to focus on more important things other than Uche Jombo weight loss or gain.
