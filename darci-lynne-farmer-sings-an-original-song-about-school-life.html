Ventriloquist and America’s Got Talent winner Darci Lynne Farmer has given her fans a glimpse of her musical process in a recent Instagram post. In the video, she can be seen strumming an electric guitar and singing a cheeky song that she wrote herself about life at school.

Darci Lynne Impresses With a New Song About School

The lyrics of Farmer’s new original song express the feeling of tiredness and boredom with schoolwork, something many students can relate to. The post is accompanied by a caption that reveals Farmer’s playful side and her ability to connect with her audience in a fun and relatable way.

The post starts with Farmer introducing her new song, which she wrote about not wanting to do school. She sings about not wanting to read Shakespeare or name all the rivers and seas. Instead, she would rather sit and stare at her guitar. The song ends with an impressive guitar solo, showcasing Farmer’s musical skills.

The young performer has been honing her musical skills since her days on AGT, where she impressed the judges with her ventriloquism and singing abilities. Since then, she has continued to perform in various venues and shows, showcasing her versatility and talent as an entertainer.

This Instagram post has once again proven that Farmer is a force to be reckoned with in the entertainment industry. Her ability to write and perform her own music adds another layer to her already impressive skill set. It’s clear that she is a rising star, and her fans can’t wait to see what she’ll do next.

Darci Lynne Attended School in Her Hometown

After attending Deer Creek Middle School near her home in Edmond, Oklahoma, until eighth grade, Farmer started homeschooling. She stopped going to school in person while launching her ventriloquism career. In March 2016, at the age of 11, she appeared on Little Big Shots with her puppet Katie, singing “I Want to Be a Cowboy’s Sweetheart.”

After winning AGT, Lynne announced a national tour, Farmer and Friends Live, beginning in January and February 2018 with five locations. The tour later expanded to 52 dates, including nine Christmas performances called Rocking Around the Christmas Tree with Darci Lynne and Friends. Lynne also made appearances on Today, Grand Ole Opry, Pickler & Ben, and Kids Baking Championship, where she performed with Petunia.

Overall, Lynne’s talent in ventriloquism and puppetry gained recognition, and she continued to showcase her skills on various stages and television programs.
