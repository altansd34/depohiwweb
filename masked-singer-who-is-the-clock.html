It’s TIME for another season 11 prediction for The Masked Singer! And this time, we’re looking at the Clock. She has an iconic voice and one of the best costumes we’ve seen so far. So with all that, she has to be the frontrunner of Group C!

So who is under the Clock mask? Here are all the clues, popular guesses, and our prediction for her true celebrity identity.

Clues on The Masked Singer Season 11

The clues for the Clock started with her saying that she’s “timeless.” Clock says that “once upon a time” she was the belle of the ball, and we see a disco ball on the screen. She had the “whole world dancing from dusk to dawn,” she claimed while rocking a baby.

But as the years passed, trends changed and she felt like she was “frozen in time.” So Clock “reinvented herself” and we see her become unfrozen by a sun. She then achieved “massive success again” on her own terms. Clock even performed for a president, she said as a golden globe spun around in front of her.

The final clue came after her performance, when one of the men in black played “Vienna” by Billy Joel on the piano. Clock explained that she has a day in her name!

Popular Guesses for The Masked Singer Clock

On the show, the judges predicted that Clock could be Anita Baker, Evelyn King, and Diana Ross. But online, most people seemed to think that this was the voice of Bette Midler.

Who is the Clock on The Masked Singer?

That voice and all the clues point to only one celebrity! And that well-known singer is…

Thelma Houston!

Now here are all the clues to back it up. We saw a disco ball and Thelma Houston is best known as a disco singer. Her story about reinventing herself also makes sense, because she left Motown and started producing albums “on her own terms.” At that same time, we saw a sun and her first album after that was called Qualifying Heat!

The spinning golden globe in her clues could be a nod to her 1975 performance at the Golden Globes. Clock mentioned singing for a president, which Thelma Houston did for Barack Obama when he accepted the Democratic nomination and at an official inauguration dinner in 2009. Finally, Thelma does have a day to her name! Thelma Houston Day was on January 29, 2003 in West Hollywood.
